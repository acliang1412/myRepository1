Short Biography

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper was an American computer scientist and United States Navy rear admiral. [3, 4] A pioneer in the field, she was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer and invented the first compiler for a computer programming language. [3] She popularized the idea of machine-independent programming languages, which led to the development of COBOL, one of the first modern programming languages. [3]

Early Life and Education

Born in New York City in 1906, Grace Brewster Murray showed her curiosity early on; at the age of seven, she famously dismantled seven alarm clocks to figure out how they worked. [12, 20] She attended Vassar College, graduating Phi Beta Kappa in 1928 with a bachelor's degree in mathematics and physics. [2, 10] She then attended Yale University, where she earned her M.A. in 1930 and a Ph.D. in mathematics in 1934. [2, 5, 7]
